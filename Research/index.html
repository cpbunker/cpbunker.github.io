Research Interests

Quantum Chemistry

Many-electron quantum mechanical systems, which are the fundamental object of quantum chemistry, exhibit a variety of conceptually difficult physics. Surprisingly, however, quantum chemists have long known the way to calculate the exact ground state of any many electron system: FCI. However, applying this method to all but the smallest systems is impossible with modern computing power. As a result, modern quantum chemistry is essentially a computing problem that consists of choosing and then applying the appropriate wavefunction ansatz.

My current work is with the Center for Molecular Magnetic Quantum Materials at UF. We use electronic structure models to study the magnetic properties of molecules. Together with experimental collaborators, we are pressing forward on how to design and control molecular qubits.

Quantum Computing

Quantum computing and quantum chemistry go hand in hand. Much effort, including my own current research, is going toward determining how chemical systems can host scalable quantum computation. However, my interest is more about how software will be developed on quantum computers as the hardware technology progresses, and how this software will transform many facets scientific research.
