Research Interests

Quantum Chemistry

In studying quantum chemistry, I apply my solid foundation in quantum mechanics to questions of electronic structure and transport in molecular systems. These systems were historically considered the domain of chemistry, but as computational methods advance they are benefitting from first principles treatments by physicists.

My current work is with the Center for Molecular Magnetic Quantum Materials at UF. We use electronic structure models to study the magnetic properties of molecules. Together with experimental collaborators, we are pressing forward on how to design and control molecular qubits.

Quantum Information

Quantum information and quantum chemistry go hand in hand. Not only is a quantum understanding of chemical systems necessary for advancing quantum computing, quantum computing methods will greatly enhance our ability to simulate systems with quantum behavior. So called Noisy Intermediate Scale Quantum (NISQ) methods are already being applied in chemistry today.
